The Problem + The Person
Where this came from

During my time as a Regulatory Affairs intern at Dabur International Ltd. in Dubai, one of my tasks was to manually visit health authority websites across multiple markets — checking for updates to herbal and health product regulations, one country at a time.

It was tedious, time-consuming, and entirely manual. Every update had to be found, read, interpreted, and logged by hand. For a function as critical as regulatory affairs, it felt like an unnecessary drain on time that could be spent on actual strategy.

That experience planted the seed. AI is ubiquitous now — it's reshaping finance, medicine, law. Regulatory Affairs, with its volume of documentation, multi-market complexity, and constant change, is exactly where it can make a real difference. Not to replace RA professionals, but to handle the tedious so they can focus on the holistic.

This tool is a small proof of that idea — built as I prepare to begin my MSc in Process Validation and Regulatory Affairs at TUS Moylish Campus, Ireland, and eager to connect with the regulatory community there.

"RA professionals shouldn't be spending their day manually refreshing health authority websites. That's exactly the kind of task AI should be handling."

— The thinking behind this project

Under the Hood
How it works

Three steps, fully automated, running every morning without any manual input.

1

Fetch

Every day at 7am UTC, the tool pulls live updates directly from official regulatory RSS feeds — no scraping, no third parties.

EMA News EMA Guidelines FDA Press Releases
2

Summarize

Each update is passed to an AI model which generates a plain-language summary — what changed and why it matters for RA teams.

Groq API Llama 3.1 30-day filter
3

Publish

The bulletin is automatically rebuilt and published as a live webpage — searchable, filterable, and always current.
